This project, OpenFunk, is licensed under the GNU LGPL version 3 or later.
My reasoning for this license change is that this means any forks have to be [free/libre software](https://fsfe.org/freesoftware/), which I believe is best for the community.
Note that this requirement (often referred to as copyleft) does not apply to the original OpenFK because of its more permissive license.

## Legal TLDR

If you have received a copy of OpenFunk or a derived work, you have a right to receive its source code.

If you have received a copy of OpenFK or NanderTGA's fork[^1] of OpenFK (also referred to as nightly builds), you don't, although it is available on their respective github repositories and in this repository's commit history.

If you have received a copy of a derivative work of OpenFK, you don't have a right to receive its source code, unless it is licensed under a license with a copyleft requirement (like is the case with OpenFunk).
If said derivative work is not licensed under a copyleft license, then it might still be available in a repository somewhere, and if not you should ask the author nicely.
